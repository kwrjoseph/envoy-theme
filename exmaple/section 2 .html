System Architecture

The Esteem Implant has three implanted components

1

Piezoelectric Sensor

Positioned on the incus bone. When the eardrum vibrates, the sensor flexes and converts these vibrations into electrical signals, sending them to the Sound Processor, effectively replacing a traditional microphone.

2

Sound Processor

Located within the temporal bone under the scalp. It receives the electrical signals and shapes them according to the unique hearing loss, before transmitting them to the Driver.

3

Piezoelectric Driver

Translates these customized signals into intensified vibrations. These vibrations are transferred to the inner ear, where the cochlea cells are stimulated.
