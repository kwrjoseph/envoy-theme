The Coverage Gap

Current Medicare policies restrict access to innovative hearing solutions, classifying them as hearing aids rather than prosthetics.

Outdated Classification

The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) currently classifies our fully implanted Esteem® device as a hearing aid, which is statutorily excluded from coverage.

Prosthetic vs. Aid

We argue that fully implanted active middle ear devices should be classified as prosthetics, which are covered benefits under the Social Security Act.

Access Denied

This classification prevents millions of seniors from accessing potentially life-changing technology that could improve their health and cognitive function.

The Hearing Device
Coverage Clarification Act

We are advocating for the passage of H.R. 1921 and S. 983. This legislation clarifies that fully implanted active middle ear devices are prosthetics.

  • Modernizes Medicare policy
  • Unlocks access for millions
  • Fosters innovation in hearing tech

Legislative Solution

By reclassifying these devices, we can align coverage with the technological reality of fully implanted prosthetics.

Why It Matters

A Public Health Crisis

Hearing loss is the third most prevalent chronic health condition in the U.S. Untreated hearing loss is linked to serious comorbidities, including:

  • Cognitive Decline & Dementia
  • Social Isolation & Depression
  • Increased Risk of Falls
