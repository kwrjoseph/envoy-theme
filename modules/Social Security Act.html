The Hearing Device Coverage Clarification Act

The Social Security Act of 1965 excluded hearing aids from Medicare and Medicaid coverage. The FDA recognizes the Esteem® fully* implanted active middle ear implant (FI-AMEI) as a prosthetic — but CMS still classifies it as a hearing aid.

This classification prevents individuals with hearing loss from accessing an alternative solution that could transform their quality of life.

How You Can Help Press Release

Outdated Classification

CMS classifies the Esteem® as a hearing aid, excluding it from Medicare coverage under the 1965 Act.

The Challenge

Breakthrough hearing technologies are unavailable to Medicare beneficiaries, hindering innovation and competition.

The Vision

This legislation would provide access to solutions for nearly 40 million Americans with disabling hearing loss.

H.R. 1921 &
S. 983

The Hearing Device Coverage Clarification Act would clarify that fully implanted active middle ear devices are Medicare-covered prosthetics — not hearing aids.

  • Modernizes Medicare hearing device policy
  • Unlocks access for millions of seniors
  • Fosters innovation and competition
