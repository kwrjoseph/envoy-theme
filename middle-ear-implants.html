How They Work

Middle ear implants are surgically implanted devices designed to assist or replace the vibrations of the ossicular chain—the three tiny bones (hammer, anvil, and stirrup) that naturally transmit sound.

By interacting directly with these structures, they provide a more natural hearing experience for patients who haven't found success with traditional amplification.

Types of Middle Ear Implants

Category A

Passive (PMEI)

Designed to bridge or replace parts of the ossicular chain that have been damaged by trauma or disease.

  • No external components
  • No internal electronics
  • Primary for Conductive Hearing Loss
Category B

Active (AMEI)

Improve hearing by directly stimulating middle ear structures using advanced electronic components.

  • Electronic stimulation
  • Addressing Mixed/Sensorineural Loss
  • Fully or Partially Implanted options
