What Is a Cochlear Implant?

A cochlear implant is an electronic medical device that replaces the function of the damaged inner ear. Unlike hearing aids, which make sounds louder, cochlear implants do the work of damaged parts of the inner ear (cochlea) to provide sound signals to the brain.

Traditional Components

  • External: Microphone, speech processor, and transmitter.
  • Internal: Receiver-stimulator and electrode array.

The Challenge

Breaking Free from External Hardware

Traditional cochlear implants have restored hearing for thousands, but they come with a compromise: the need to wear external processors and microphones on the ear.

This external hardware can limit lifestyle activities, create stigma, and restrict hearing to only when the device is worn.

Technology

How Acclaim® Works

1

Sensor

The piezoelectric sensor is attached to the ossicles. It picks up vibrations from the eardrum, utilizing the natural acoustics of the outer ear.

2

Processor

An implanted sound processor filters and converts these mechanical vibrations into digital electrical signals, customized to the patient's needs.

3

Electrode Array

The signals are transmitted to the cochlea via an electrode array, directly stimulating the auditory nerve and bypassing damaged hair cells.
