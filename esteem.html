Fully Implanted Active Middle Ear Implant (FI-AMEI)

The Esteem®
Implant

The first and only FDA-approved, fully implanted, active middle ear implant. Invisible hearing without external components.

Designed for adults with moderate to severe sensorineural hearing loss who desire a unique, 24/7 hearing solution that uses the ear's natural anatomy.

The Esteem device is different. It is invisible. You don't wear it; you live with it.

Unlike hearing aids or cochlear implants, the Esteem is fully implanted under the skin. It eliminates the artificial microphone found in other devices, instead using your own eardrum to capture sound naturally. This innovative design reduces the background noise and acoustic feedback often associated with traditional hearing aids.

  • **No external components** to put on or take off.
  • **Maintenance-free** daily life—no cleaning or battery changing.
  • **Waterproof**: Swim, shower, and exercise freely.
  • **Natural Sound**: Uses the ear canal and eardrum.

How it Works

The Esteem system uses three fully implanted components to mechanically stimulate the middle ear, bypassing the damaged parts of the cochlea.

1. The Sensor

The piezoelectric sensor is attached to the incus (anvil) bone. It picks up the physical vibrations from the eardrum.

By using the eardrum as a microphone, it preserves the ear's natural ability to localize sound and filter noise.

2. The Sound Processor

Implanted behind the ear, this device filters, processes, and amplifies the electrical signals from the sensor.

It houses the battery, which lasts approximately 4.5 to 9 years depending on usage.

3. The Driver

Attached to the stapes (stirrup) bone, the piezoelectric driver converts the confirmed electrical signals back into mechanical vibrations.

These intensified vibrations are delivered directly to the inner ear, allowing for clear, natural sound perception.

Is Esteem Right for You?

The Esteem implant is a prescription device. Candidacy is determined by a certified hearing specialist, but generalized criteria include:

General Candidacy Criteria

  • Adults 18 years of age or older.
  • Stable moderate to severe sensorineural hearing loss in both ears.
  • Unaided word recognition score of 40% or better.
  • At least 30 days of experience with appropriately fit hearing aids.
  • Normally functioning Eustachian tube and middle ear anatomy.
