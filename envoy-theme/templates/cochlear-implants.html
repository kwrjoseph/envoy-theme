A cochlear implant is an inner ear implant designed to restore the ability to understand speech and perceive sounds in individuals with moderate to profound sensorineural hearing loss who are no longer benefiting from traditional amplification.

The device has two main parts:

  • External: Microphone, speech processor, and transmitter.
  • Internal: Electrode array and receiver-stimulator.

External components must be removed for sleeping, showering, and swimming.

Cochlear implant candidacy criteria vary slightly by the implant manufacturer.
