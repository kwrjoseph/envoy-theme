Hearing loss is not the same for everyone. It is typically categorized into four distinct degrees.

  1. Mild — Ability to hear parts of speech, but difficulty with softer sounds.
  2. Moderate — Struggle to hear speech at a normal level.
  3. Severe — Inability to hear most speech at normal levels; ability to hear loud sounds.
  4. Profound — Inability to hear any normal speech; ability to only hear very loud sounds.
