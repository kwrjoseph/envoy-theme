Despite major advancements in medical technology, individuals with hearing loss have not benefited from the same level of innovation and access. Hearing loss is the third most common chronic health condition in the United States, yet Medicare’s outdated classification restricts access to breakthrough solutions.

Why the Current Policy Is a Problem

  • Outdated Classification — CMS categorizes the Esteem® implant as a hearing aid, automatically excluding it from Medicare coverage.
  • The Challenge — Medicare beneficiaries are blocked from innovative options that could significantly improve quality of life.
  • The Vision — This legislation would open access to modern hearing technologies for nearly 40 million Americans with disabling hearing loss.

Passing this legislation will require broad public support — and your voice can help move it forward.

Blackwell DL, Lucas JW, Clarke TC. Summary health statistics for US adults: National Health Interview Survey, 2012. Vital Health Stat 10. 2014;260:1-161.

Source: NIDCD Quick Statistics on Hearing
