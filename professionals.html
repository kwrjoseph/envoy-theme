How the Esteem works

Three core implanted components working in harmony with human anatomy.

1

Piezoelectric Sensor

Positioned on the incus bone. When the eardrum vibrates, the sensor captures these movements and converts them into electrical signals.

2

Sound Processor

Subcutaneously implanted behind the ear. It processes signals based on the patient's unique hearing profile and transmits them to the driver.

3

Piezoelectric Driver

Converts processed signals back into mechanical vibrations, directly stimulating the stapes bone for natural hearing perception.

Who is a Candidate?

The Esteem is designed for adults with moderate to severe sensorineural hearing loss. Use our clinical criteria to identify potential candidates in your clinic.

18 years of age or older
Stable bilateral sensorineural hearing loss
Moderate to severe SNHL (defined by PTA)
Unaided SDS Score ≥ 40%
Normally functioning Eustachian tube
Normal middle ear anatomy & membrane
Adequate space (determined via CT scan)
Min. 30 days hearing aid experience

INDICATIONS: The Esteem® is indicated for use in adults, 18 years of age or older, who have stable bilateral sensorineural hearing loss and can benefit from amplification.

CONTRAINDICATIONS: The Esteem is contraindicated for patients with chronic middle ear infections, inner ear disorders like Meniere's, or any medical condition that prevents surgery.

ADVERSE EVENTS: As with any surgical procedure, there are risks involved. Potential adverse events may include infection, facial nerve weakness, or change in taste perception.
